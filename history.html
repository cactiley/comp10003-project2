2014

Elan lee conceptualises the idea to create a game that allows people to interact and engage with each other Him and his friend Shane Small create “bomb squad” a game with bomb cards that eventually explode. They approach Matthew Inman from “The Oatmeal” with their idea.

Jan 2015

Matthew Inman loves the idea but worries that the subject of bombs might be too intense. Inman and Lee decide to centre the game around kittens instead! Together, they launch a crowdfunded Kickstarter campaign, with intent to gather enough funds to produce 10000 decks. On January 20th, Exploding Kittens was announced as a Kickstarter project.

Feb 2015

By the end of the campaign on February 19th, the game had raised $8,782,571 (USD) from 219,382 backers. Significantly exceeding their goal, Exploding Kittens become the most backed campaign in Kickstarter history.

Jan-Apr 2016

The IOS version of the game is released in January 2016. After popular demand, an android version of the Exploding Kittens mobile game is released in April.

Oct 2016

The first expansion kit is released; the Imploding Kittens card set, and is incredibly well received. This expansion adds 20 new cards to the game, as well as a novel “cone of shame”, and the Imploding Kitten.

2017

The game team has expanded to 30 full time members.

2022

A free-to-play Exploding Kittens online game, backed and created by Netflix, is released.

2024

A Netflix animated 9 episode Exploding Kittens show premiers, supported by Netflix and Bandara Entertainment. This adult animated comedy series follows a tale of “Godcat” and “Devilcat”, sent to Earth and trapped in the bodies of cats.
